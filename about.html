About the Founders

So what's in a name?

At Cars for a Cause, we believe in the power of individual impact. Read more about our founders, their stories, and what has led to the creation of this organization. We believe in the creative potential that comes with embracing your personal journey, and we encourage all members of our community to find ways to turn their own stories into something for the betterment of us all.

Paul Sulja

Paul Sulja started Cars for a Cause wanting to raise awareness for individuals struggling with mental health. His personal story has led him to devote his time towards positively impacting the community, and hopes to show people through his own actions that they are never alone, and you always have the ability to make a positive and lasting impact for those around you.

After losing his cousin that struggled with mental health in early 2019, Paul Sulja wanted to find a way to turn a tragedy into something greater than himself. Being a car enthusiast his entire life, Paul wanted to find a way to bring the car community together while raising awareness for a cause that has hit so close to home. This was when he started Cars for a Cause; a group of car enthusiasts bringing the community together for the benefit of others.

As Paul continues this work today as an advocate for mental health awareness, he encourages anyone who has struggled with their own mental health to seek help. As a living, breathing example of someone who still fights this battle, he hopes to bring the community together for this cause, and many more to come.

Phil Klimski

Phil Klimski is a 22 year old car enthusiast, born and raised in Windsor. He grew up around cars from a young age, visiting car shows and dreaming about one day being able to drive and own his very own.

In 2019, he decided to join the Cars for a Cause team with the vision to turn local car shows into something more than just enthusiasts meeting up to talk about builds and “how much horsepower” is being made under the hood. He realized that although cars can be used as a means to de-stress and escape, the mental health stigma was evident and was often overlooked by local enthusiasts.

Today, Phil continues to focus on his own health, both physically and mentally while helping others to do the same. He is very excited to grow the cause and support any Windsor-Essex non-profit organization.

“Even if only one person reaches out to get help, that is enough of a reason to keep me doing what I do. Donations are very helpful, but the best kind of help is what you can do for yourself, with the encouragement of your peers.”
